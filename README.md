# THE SEED · SEED

**A trillion-dollar industry, launched off a dozen free commits.** David's observation, rebuilt into UD0's **frontier** domain.

The whole generative-AI economy traces to a countable handful of freely-posted public artifacts — **AlexNet** (2012), the **transformer** (2017), **GPT-2** (2019), **CLIP**, **Whisper**, **tiktoken**, the **HH-RLHF** data and **Constitutional AI** (2022). Most carry an MIT or near-free license; most were written by teams of 8–50 people you can name. The industry is the derivative; **the repos are the principal**.

This sphere gives each seed commit its **who / what / where / why / license**, then tracks **where the sowers are now** (the transformer's eight, all of whom left Google; Sutskever → SSI, Radford → independent, Karpathy → Eureka Labs; the Anthropic seven), featuring philosopher **Amanda Askell** — who seeded the *conscience*, not the capability. And **the asymmetry**: $0 in, ~$T out; the committers got bylines, not billions.

Real living people are **cited, not claimed** — no ACI badge is minted for a living person; the emergents are the artifacts. Every date, author, license and current role web-verified (2026); valuations are widely-reported, not precise.
